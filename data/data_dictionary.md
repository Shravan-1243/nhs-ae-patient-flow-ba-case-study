# Data Dictionary & Sourcing Notes

## Purpose

This file documents exactly where every number in this project comes from. It exists so anyone reviewing this case study — a recruiter, a hiring manager, a fellow analyst — can see precisely which figures are real, official statistics and which are illustrative trust-level data built for this portfolio exercise.

## What's real vs. illustrative

**Real (official, citable, public):**
- National 4-hour A&E breach rate: 36.2% in March 2026
- National waiting list: ~7.1–7.2 million cases (March/April 2026)
- National A&E attendances: record 2.5 million in May 2026
- National 12-hour trolley waits: 50,000+ in May 2026 (17% YoY increase)
- National nursing vacancy rate: 6.7% (December 2025)

Sources:
- House of Commons Library, *NHS key statistics: England*, briefing CBP-7281 (accessed June 2026): https://commonslibrary.parliament.uk/research-briefings/cbp-7281/
- Nuffield Trust, *QualityWatch — NHS Performance Dashboard* (accessed June 2026): https://www.nuffieldtrust.org.uk/qualitywatch/nhs-performance-dashboard
- NHS England, *A&E Attendances and Emergency Admissions 2025-26* statistical work area: https://www.england.nhs.uk/statistics/statistical-work-areas/ae-waiting-times-and-activity/

All of the above are published under the Open Government Licence v3.0.

**Illustrative (built for this case study):**

The file `data/trust_ae_performance_apr25_mar26.csv` represents a single fictional trust ("Thameswood NHS Foundation Trust") and was generated by `data/build_dataset.py`. It is **not** an extract from any real trust's system. It was built by:

1. Taking the real national monthly breach-rate trend shape (worsening through winter, peaking in the 36–44% range, consistent with the cited sources above)
2. Applying a realistic trust-level adjustment (+2.4 to +5.9 percentage points above the national average in winter months), consistent with commentary from Nuffield Trust and NHS Confederation on the spread between best- and worst-performing Type 1 departments
3. Modelling attendance volumes, admission rates, and nursing vacancy on a seasonal curve typical of a mid-sized acute Type 1 department (~210–230 attendances/day average)

This approach was chosen over downloading and using a single real trust's raw provider-level CSV (also published openly by NHS England) for two reasons:
- It avoids attributing genuinely poor performance to a real, named NHS Trust in a portfolio piece, which would be both unfair to that organisation and a misleading product to show an employer
- It allows the case study to demonstrate the full BA toolkit (problem framing, stakeholder analysis, requirements, process redesign) against a clean, realistic dataset without spending the limited project time on data cleaning of a real multi-sheet provider file

## If you want to extend this with real provider-level data

NHS England publishes monthly, trust-by-trust A&E performance data as open CSV/XLS files here:
https://www.england.nhs.uk/statistics/statistical-work-areas/ae-waiting-times-and-activity/

Each monthly release includes total attendances, 4-hour performance, and emergency admissions broken down by individual NHS Trust. This would be the natural next step to convert this from an illustrative case study into a live-data analysis project.

## Column definitions — `trust_ae_performance_apr25_mar26.csv`

| Column | Description |
|---|---|
| `month` | Calendar month label (e.g. "Apr-2025") |
| `month_start` | First day of the month, ISO format |
| `total_attendances` | Total A&E attendances in the month (all A&E types) |
| `seen_within_4hrs` | Attendances admitted, transferred, or discharged within 4 hours |
| `breached_4hr_target` | Attendances exceeding the 4-hour standard |
| `four_hr_breach_pct` | `breached_4hr_target` ÷ `total_attendances` × 100 |
| `twelve_hr_trolley_waits` | Patients waiting over 12 hours post decision-to-admit |
| `emergency_admissions` | Total emergency admissions in the month |
| `admission_rate_pct` | `emergency_admissions` ÷ `total_attendances` × 100 |
| `nursing_vacancy_pct` | Modelled nursing vacancy rate for the department, illustrative |
